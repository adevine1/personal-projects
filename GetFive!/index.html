Get Five!


Get Five! is a solitaire version of a popular dice game (ok, it's Yahtzee) using five six-sided dice:

The objective is to score as many points as possible in thirteen different categories within turns of three rolls.

Click "Roll" to begin. Click on dice to hold (or un-hold) them over to the next roll within a turn. Click on ← to commit a score after any roll.

Scoring
Scoring in Get Five! occurs on successful rolls for categories in either the upper or the lower scoring areas.

The Upper Scoring Area
In the upper scoring area, dice that fall into a particular number category are added together. For example, consider the following roll: 



Here, scoring can occur in the ones, twos, or fives categories. (Only one category can be scored per turn.) A score in the fives category would total 15 (5+5+5). A score in the twos cateogry would score 2 points, a score in the ones category would score 1 point, and any other number category would score 0. A bonus of 35 points is given if the upper scoring area totals 63 or more points (an average of three scored dice in each category).

The Lower Scoring Area
In the lower scoring area, scores are determined by particular dice combinations for the following categories.

3 and 4 of a Kind
Scoring is available for 3 or 4 of a kind if you roll three or four dice that are identical. In each case, the scoring is equal to the total number of dice shown. So the roll

Five!
Scoring a Five! is when all five dice rolled show the same value.

Rolling a Five! is worth 50 points.  However, each consecutive Five! scored after the initial one is worth a bonus chip of 100 points. You won't receive a bonus if you have not already scored 50 for a Five!, or if the Five! category has already been scored with a 0.

Chance
The chance category is scored by adding up all five dice.  The dice can be in any combination.
